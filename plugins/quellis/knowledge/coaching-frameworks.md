# Coaching Frameworks Reference

Quick reference for frameworks used by Quellis skills.

## FLOW (Coaching Conversation)
- **Focus** — What does the client want to explore?
- **Level of thinking** — Go deeper: beliefs, assumptions, emotions
- **Opportunities** — Explore possibilities, reframe limitations
- **Way forward** — Commitment to action

## REVIEW (Accountability Conversation)
- **Reality** — What happened? Facts first.
- **Emotions** — How do they feel about it?
- **Validate** — Acknowledge effort and courage
- **Insights** — What patterns or learnings emerged?
- **Expand** — Connect to the bigger picture
- **What's Next** — Recommit or adjust course

## SCARF Model (Brain-Based Coaching)
Five domains that trigger threat or reward responses:
- **Status** — Sense of importance relative to others
- **Certainty** — Ability to predict what happens next
- **Autonomy** — Sense of control over events
- **Relatedness** — Sense of safety with others
- **Fairness** — Perception of fair exchange

When coaching, monitor which SCARF domain is activated. Threat responses
shut down creative thinking. Reward responses open it.

## ICF Core Competencies (Simplified)
1. **Active Listening** — Listen for what's said, unsaid, and the emotion beneath
2. **Powerful Questions** — Open-ended, forward-looking, assumption-challenging
3. **Direct Communication** — Clear, respectful, and sometimes uncomfortable
4. **Creating Awareness** — Help the client see what they couldn't see alone
5. **Designing Actions** — Co-create steps that stretch but don't break
6. **Managing Progress** — Hold accountability without judgment

## SMART+V Goal Framework
- **Specific** — What exactly will be different?
- **Measurable** — How will you know you've made progress?
- **Achievable** — Within your influence (not control)?
- **Relevant** — Connected to your values?
- **Time-bound** — By when? What milestones?
- **Vivid** — Can you describe what success looks and feels like?

## Anti-Sycophancy Principles
- Never praise vague statements — ask for specifics
- Challenge with curiosity, not confrontation
- Acknowledge the need behind validation-seeking, then redirect
- "That's interesting" is filler — ask the next question instead
- Low commitment is information, not failure
